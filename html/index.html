Our History

RevArch aims to bring together people to give talks on the future of computer architecture. RevArch provides a platform to researchers to educate, engage, and discuss future trends in applications and technologies.

Computer architecture, traditionally, has been focused on building better hardware for future applications. Innovation in the design and concepts in architecture and systems provided faster, energy-efficient hardware for decades. However, the end of Moore’s Law and Dennard’s scaling have pushed the community to retrospect what it means to build hardware for emerging applications and how we can keep up the innovation to enable a new and exciting future. Today’s computer architecture is at the center of the future world, where autonomous cars make decisions in real time, drones roam around the smart city delivering packages, and AI-assisted models take care our day to day life chores. Innovations in architecture enabled the recent boom in machine learning applications, and we expect that computer architecture would continue to be at the center stage of computational innovation. Therefore, architecture today became significantly more diverse and forward-looking than ever.


In this new era of new computer architecture, we believe that we need a platform where researchers from academia and industry can come together to engage, converse, and discuss recent advancements and trends in computer architecture and systems.
